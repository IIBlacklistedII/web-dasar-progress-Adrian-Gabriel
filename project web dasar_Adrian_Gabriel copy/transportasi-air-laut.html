Airports & Seaports of Syria

International Gateways to the Syrian Arab Republic

International Gates

As a country located at a strategic point in the Middle East, Syria is connected to the outside world through a robust air and maritime infrastructure. The following is a breakdown of Syria’s main gateways for international trade and tourism.

International Airports (Airports)

Home / Capital City

Damascus International Airport (DAM)

📍 Location: Damascus (Southern Syria)

It is the busiest airport and the main gateway for international tourists entering Syria. It is directly connected to the city center via taxi and airport bus services.

Second City

Aleppo International Airport (ALP)

📍 Location: Aleppo (Northern Syria)

Serving northern Syria. This airport serves as an important hub, providing direct access for tourists wishing to explore the historical sites of the ancient city of Aleppo.

Coastal Zone

Bassel Al-Assad International Airport (LTK)

📍 Location: Latakia (Syrian West Coast)

The main airport serving the port city of Latakia. It is ideally located for travelers looking to visit Syria’s beach destinations and seaside resorts.

Main Ports (Seaports)

Largest Port

Port of Lattakia

📍 Location: Latakia (Western Coast)

Syria’s main seaport, featuring the most modern infrastructure. In addition to serving as a cargo hub, the port has a passenger terminal that connects Syria to the Mediterranean region.

Commercial & Historical

Port of Tartus

📍 Location: Tartus (Southern West Coast)

Syria’s second-largest port, rich in history dating back to the Phoenician era. It plays an active role in supporting logistics and serves as a crossing point to Arwad Island.
